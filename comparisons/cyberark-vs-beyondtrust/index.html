CyberArk vs. BeyondTrust for NHI: What Your PAM Actually Covers

If you're already running CyberArk or BeyondTrust for PAM, your vendor will tell you it covers non-human identities too. That's true. The important question is how much it covers, and whether what's left uncovered is where your actual NHI risk lives.

Framing this comparison correctly

This comparison is about using PAM platforms for NHI governance, not about PAM selection for privileged human access. If you're choosing between CyberArk and BeyondTrust for PAM, that's a different evaluation. The question here is narrower: if you run one of these platforms, how far does it take you on NHI, and when do you need something purpose-built regardless?

What CyberArk actually delivers on NHI

CyberArk has the most comprehensive machine identity portfolio of any PAM vendor. The Venafi acquisition brought the CLM market leader into the stack. Conjur covers enterprise secrets management. Workload Identity Manager adds SPIFFE-based ephemeral identity for cloud workloads. The 2025 Secure Workload Access Solution attempts to unify these under a single view for multi-cloud machine identity governance.

The integration is real but not complete. CyberArk acquired Venafi in 2024 and the post-acquisition roadmap is being built in real time. When you evaluate CyberArk for NHI, ask specifically which product layer covers your use case and don't assume the different components already operate as a unified platform. They're moving in that direction. They're not there yet.

What BeyondTrust actually delivers on NHI

BeyondTrust's NHI coverage is narrower and more honest about its scope. Password Safe covers automated discovery and management of privileged accounts including service accounts and application credentials in environments where BeyondTrust already manages privileged human access. That's a meaningful extension of the PAM model.

The scope limit is clear: BeyondTrust doesn't cover certificates, workload identity, SaaS OAuth tokens, or AI agent credentials. For organizations whose NHI problem is primarily service account sprawl and application credential rotation in on-premises environments under existing PAM management, Password Safe covers a real portion of the problem. For organizations with modern cloud environments, SaaS integration sprawl, or AI agent deployment, it doesn't.

What neither covers

This matters more than the comparison between the two platforms. Both share the same structural limitation: they govern NHIs within the perimeter of their PAM deployment, not the broader universe of credentials across cloud, SaaS, and development environments.

Neither platform finds the OAuth token granted to a third-party SaaS integration by a developer three years ago. Neither finds the API key hardcoded in a CI/CD pipeline. Neither governs AI agent credentials that weren't created through a formal provisioning process. For those scenarios, purpose-built NHI governance platforms go considerably further.

The right question for PAM platform evaluations isn't which PAM covers NHI better. It's: what NHI coverage gap remains after my PAM investment, and what do I need to close it?

Verdict

CyberArk is the stronger choice if machine identity is a significant program requirement and you're willing to navigate the post-Venafi integration complexity. The CLM depth and workload identity capability are genuine advantages BeyondTrust doesn't have. BeyondTrust is a reasonable NHI extension for organizations whose NHI risk is primarily service account governance in environments already under BeyondTrust PAM management. Neither is a substitute for purpose-built NHI governance when your exposure includes SaaS integration sprawl, cloud IAM proliferation, or AI agent identity. Know what your PAM covers. Then figure out what it doesn't.
